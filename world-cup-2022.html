Research Overview

The 2022 FIFA World Cup in Qatar was a landmark event that reshaped global football politics and placed the Gulf region at the centre of international sport diplomacy. Dr. Amara has been a leading scholarly voice on its political, cultural, and social dimensions — from the bidding process and labour governance debates to its legacy for Qatari society and regional sport development.

His research examines Qatar 2022 as a prism through which to understand broader questions of sport nationalism, the Gulf developmental state, postcolonial aspirations, Islam and global sport, and the geopolitics of mega-event hosting.

As co-editor of the first comprehensive academic volume on the tournament, and through keynote addresses at major international forums, Dr. Amara has shaped the scholarly conversation around the tournament's legacies.
